      Journey — Visitor Flow & Drop-off Map

      Exit Pages & Drop-off

      Pulse — Visitor Cadence & Traffic Timing

      When do visitors actually show up? This reconstructs the weekly rhythm of site entries from pageview timestamps — peak hours, busiest days, and how often people arrive. Useful for timing commission openings, drops, and posts. Derived live from analytics data (visitor local time) — no extra tracking, no new storage key.

      Weekly Punchcard

      Each cell is one hour of one weekday. Warmer cells mean more visits land in that slot — your recurring traffic windows surface here.

      Busiest Days

      Total site entries per day of the week.

      Peak Hours

      Hours of the day ranked by entries. The top rows are when your audience is most likely browsing.

      Compass — Traffic Sources & Acquisition

      Where does your audience actually come from? This groups every visit by its referrer into channels — social, search, direct, and other referrals — and surfaces your top referring sites and device mix. Useful for knowing which platform (X, Instagram, ArtStation, TikTok…) is sending real traffic so you can post where it lands. Derived live from analytics pageviews — no new storage key.

      Channel Mix

      Every visit bucketed by acquisition channel. Direct = typed/bookmarked, Social = X/Instagram/TikTok/ArtStation/etc., Search = Google/Bing/etc., Referral = any other linking site.

      Top Referrers

      The actual sites sending visitors, by hostname. Direct visits (no referrer) are listed too so you can see how much traffic is brand-driven.

      Device Mix

      Desktop vs. mobile vs. tablet across all visits — a check that the portfolio reads well on whatever your audience actually uses.

      Top Screen Sizes

      Most common visitor screen resolutions, helpful for deciding which breakpoints to polish first.

      Depth — Scroll Reach & Read-Through

      How far down each page do visitors actually scroll before they leave? Compass tells you where traffic comes from and Journey where it goes between pages — Depth shows the vertical story: which pages get read to the bottom and which lose people above the fold. Derived live from the scroll-depth events analytics already records — no new storage key. Note: a session that never scrolls (short page or instant bounce) counts as 0% reach, so treat reach as relative engagement, not a literal pixel measure.

      Read-Through Funnel by Page

      For each page, the share of visitors who reached 25% / 50% / 75% / 100% of the page. A healthy funnel narrows gently; a sharp drop between two tiers marks where attention falls off.

      Where Readers Drop Off

      Pages ranked by the share of visitors who left before reaching the halfway point — your weakest above-the-fold hooks. High numbers here are candidates for a stronger opening or shorter layout.

      Average Scroll Depth by Page

      Mean furthest-point reached per visit, page by page. The deepest pages are the ones holding attention all the way through.

      Orbit — Returning Visitors & Loyalty

      Every other analytics tool keys off a per-tab session, so each visit looks brand-new. Orbit adds a persistent visitor id (stored in the browser) so you can finally tell a first-timer from someone who keeps coming back — how loyal your audience is, how often they return, and how recently. The single best signal that your work is building a following rather than just catching one-off clicks. Derived live from pageviews; the visitor id is the only new storage key.

      New vs. Returning

      First-time visitors (a single recorded visit) versus returning ones (two or more). A healthy, growing portfolio steadily grows the returning slice.

      Visit Frequency

      How many times visitors have come back, bucketed. A long tail toward the higher buckets means real regulars — people checking for new work, not just passing through once.

      Recency — Last Seen

      When each visitor was last here. A wall of "today / this week" means an active, warm audience; a pile in "30+ days" means people drifted off and could be worth a re-engagement post.

      Most Loyal Visitors

      Your top returning visitors by visit count, with how long they've been following and when they last dropped in. These are the people your next drop should be aimed at first.

      Beacon — Conversion Funnel & Goal Tracking

      Every other tool measures attention; Beacon measures outcome — how many visitors actually travel the path to a commission inquiry. It reconstructs the funnel from landing → viewing commissions → opening the form → reaching the contact step → submitting, and shows exactly where would-be clients fall away. Derived live from pageviews and a handful of lightweight goal events the form fires — no new storage key. Note: reach is per session, so a single visitor across several tabs may count more than once.

      Conversion Funnel

      Each stage as a share of all sessions. A healthy funnel narrows gently; a steep cliff between two stages marks the step that loses the most prospective clients — your highest-leverage fix.

      Biggest Drop-off Points

      Stage transitions ranked by the share of visitors lost between them. The top row is where you bleed the most commissions — worth a clearer call-to-action, a shorter form, or stronger reassurance at that exact point.

      Commission CTA Clicks by Page

      Where visitors click a "commission me / inquiry" call-to-action across the site. Tells you which pages are doing the persuading — and which could use a CTA they're currently missing.

      Which Sources Convert

      Of the channels that send traffic, which actually produce inquiries. A source with lots of visits but no conversions is reach without intent; one converting above its visit share is where your real clients come from.

      Recent Inquiries

      The most recent completed submissions, with the commission type and when they landed. Cross-check against the Inquiries tab for the full message.

      Relay — Outbound Reach & Link Clicks

      Compass answers where visitors come from; Relay is its mirror — where they go when they click a link off the portfolio. Which social platform, shop, or external destination actually pulls the click-throughs, and which page sends them. The single clearest signal of which off-site profile the portfolio is driving traffic to. Derived live from the click events analytics already records (each carries its link target) — no new storage key.

      Destination Mix

      Every outbound click split by kind of destination: social platforms, your shops & projects (Yuka, NYFurs, commission form…), other external sites, and email. Tells you at a glance whether the portfolio is feeding your socials, your storefronts, or leaking to unrelated links.

      Top Destinations

      Ranked list of the external destinations visitors click through to. This is where the portfolio is actually sending your audience — the profiles and shops worth keeping current, and the ones nobody is clicking.

      Where They Leave From

      Which pages generate the most outbound clicks. A page sending lots of traffic off-site is doing its referral job; one with none may be missing the links your audience is looking for.

      Recent Outbound Clicks

      The latest link-outs, with destination, the page they left from, and when. A quick pulse on what's catching attention right now.
